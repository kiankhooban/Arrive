# Canadian Settlement Resources Database
## Housing, Legal Aid & Employment — Ontario & BC (Verified Active 2026)

> **How to use this document:** All entries have been verified against official healthline directories, 211 databases, organization websites, and government sources. URLs point to direct service pages where available. Phone numbers are as of the most recent update indicated in each source. Organizations are IRCC-funded settlement service providers (SPOs) unless otherwise noted.

## SECTION 1: HOUSING

### 1A — Housing (Ontario)

***

**1. Matthew House Toronto — Emergency Shelter & Transitional Housing**
- **Description:** Provides emergency shelter (reception house) and transitional housing across six homes in Toronto for newly arrived refugee claimants, including specialized homes for youth and unaccompanied minors.[^1][^2]
- **Province:** Ontario
- **Eligibility:** Refugee claimants in Canada less than 28 days, including unaccompanied minors; transitional program for vulnerable refugee claimants (adults, youth, and children).[^2]
- **Category:** Housing — Emergency Shelter / Transitional
- **URL:** https://www.matthewhouse.ca/what-we-do
- **Phone:** 416-981-5151 (admin: admin@matthewhouse.ca)
- **Notes:** Capacity 48 (reception house). No fee. Referral or self-referral. Does NOT serve permanent residents or those with PR status — restricted to refugee claimants. Services in English; referrals available for interpretation.[^3][^2]

***

**2. Sojourn House — Emergency Shelter & Transitional Housing**
- **Description:** One of Toronto's longest-running refugee shelters, providing emergency shelter (94 single adults + 225 families nightly), transitional housing (52 units up to 2 years), and outreach housing supports.[^4][^5]
- **Province:** Ontario
- **Eligibility:** Newly arrived refugees and refugee claimants; families referred through Toronto Central Intake (416-338-4766); single persons may apply directly.[^5]
- **Category:** Housing — Emergency Shelter / Transitional
- **URL:** https://www.sojournhouse.org
- **Phone:** 416-864-0515 ext. 221 or 222
- **Notes:** Main shelter at 101 Ontario St; satellite shelter at 250 Queen St E (34 single men); family shelter at 165 Grange Ave (approx. 225 persons, 78 rooms). In-house health clinic for residents. Youth program for ages 16–24.[^6][^5]

***

**3. Romero House — Transitional Housing & Settlement Support**
- **Description:** Offers transitional housing (19 units across 5 houses, up to 12 months) with wraparound settlement support including legal process assistance, social assistance, employment and education supports, and a weekly food bank.[^7][^8]
- **Province:** Ontario
- **Eligibility:** Refugee claimants and undocumented peoples; walk-in program also open to refugee claimants not residing at Romero House for settlement referrals and housing search support.[^9][^8]
- **Category:** Housing — Transitional / Settlement Support
- **URL:** https://romerohouse.org
- **Phone:** 416-763-1303
- **Notes:** Intake hours Mon–Wed 10 am–12:30 pm, 1:30–4 pm; Thu–Fri by appointment. Capacity up to 80 people. Serves refugee claimants, temporary residents, convention refugees, PRs, citizens, and undocumented persons. Available in French.[^10][^9]

***

**4. FCJ Refugee Centre — Transitional Housing (Women & Children)**
- **Description:** Provides transitional housing specifically for refugee claimant women and children (capacity 30, stays up to 12 months), paired with legal support, mental health referrals, and social services accompaniment.[^11][^12]
- **Province:** Ontario
- **Eligibility:** Refugee claimant women and children; precarious migrants and refugee populations. New clients drop in Mondays 9:30 am–4 pm.[^12][^11]
- **Category:** Housing — Transitional (Women & Children)
- **URL:** https://www.fcjrefugeecentre.org
- **Phone:** 416-469-9754
- **Notes:** Also offers primary health care (no OHIP required), English language learning for people without status, food distribution, and legal/immigration case management. Languages: Arabic, English, French, Hindi, Portuguese, Serbian, Spanish, Tibetan, Urdu.[^13][^12]

***

**5. COSTI Immigrant Services — Housing Access Program**
- **Description:** Housing workers assist individuals and families at risk of homelessness with accessing affordable market-rent and subsidized housing, advocacy with Toronto Community Housing, and access to the COSTI Rent Bank Program.[^14][^15]
- **Province:** Ontario
- **Eligibility:** Anyone in the North York area who is under-housed, at risk of homelessness, and faces cultural, linguistic, or socioeconomic barriers to housing — including immigrants and refugees.[^14]
- **Category:** Housing — Housing Help Centre / Affordable Rental
- **URL:** https://www.costi.org/programs/program_details.php?sid=0&pid=0&id=226
- **Phone:** 416-244-0480
- **Notes:** Drop-in hours Mon–Fri 1–5 pm; Wed and Fri 9:30–11:30 am. Located at Sheridan Mall, 1700 Wilson Ave, Suite 114, Toronto. Languages include Albanian, Arabic, Farsi, French, Greek, Italian, Polish, Turkish, Russian, Spanish. Funded by City of Toronto and United Way.[^14]

- **Province:** Ontario
- **Eligibility:** Homeless or at-risk individuals and families, with a focus on immigrants and refugees facing cultural, linguistic, and socioeconomic barriers.[^15]
- **Category:** Housing — Housing Help Centre
- **URL:** https://www.costi.org/supportingcommunity/housing.php
- **Phone:** 416-244-0480

**7. WoodGreen Community Services — Housing Access Centre**

- **Province:** Ontario
- **Eligibility:** Newcomers, seniors, youth, people with disabilities, and people experiencing homelessness. Includes immigrants, convention refugees, and permanent residents.[^19]
- **Category:** Housing — Affordable / Transitional Housing
- **URL:** https://www.woodgreen.org/programs/housing-access-centre
- **Phone:** 416-645-6000 (general)

**9. Toronto Rent Bank — Neighbourhood Information Post (NIP)**
- **Description:** Provides one-time grants of up to $4,000 (or 3 months' rent) for Toronto residents who are behind on rent or need help with a first/last month's rent deposit.[^22][^23]
- **Province:** Ontario
- **Eligibility:** Toronto residents (including newcomers) who are paying market rent, have a source of income, are not on Ontario Works (OW) or ODSP, and whose household income is within threshold (e.g., max $46,500 for individual). Must be on lease or moving to Toronto.[^23][^24]
- **Category:** Housing — Rent Bank / Financial Assistance
- **URL:** https://nipost.org/toronto-rent-bank/
- **Phone:** 416-397-7368 (416-397-RENT)
- **Notes:** Accessible through NIP and multiple Local Access Centres including COSTI, West Toronto Community Legal Services, Albion Neighbourhood Services, Unison Health, and others. Application can be made online or in person. Funded in part by Mayor Chow's 2026 budget ($10.8M allocated).[^25][^16]

## USAGE NOTES FOR DATABASE IMPORT

### Eligibility Status Codes (Suggested JSON Schema)
- `refugee_claimant` — Persons who have filed or intend to file a refugee claim at the IRB
- `convention_refugee` / `protected_person` — Persons recognized as refugees (positive decision)
- `government_assisted_refugee` (GAR) — Resettled refugees sponsored by the government
- `permanent_resident` (PR) — Persons with PR status
- `work_permit_holder` — Temporary foreign workers
- `study_permit_holder` — International students
- `all_newcomers` — Any of the above
- `undocumented` / `no_status` — Persons without valid immigration documents

### Key Restrictions to Note
1. **Many Employment Ontario bridging programs (JVS, YMCA OBTP, ACCES) require work authorization** — refugee claimants who have received a work permit are eligible, but those still waiting are often NOT.
2. **YMCA Mentoring for Employment** is restricted to PRs, convention refugees, and protected persons only — not refugee claimants.
3. **Romero House and FCJ** serve refugee claimants regardless of status and do not require a LAO certificate for basic services.
4. **Matthew House** is strictly limited to refugee claimants in Canada less than 28 days for the reception shelter.
5. **MOSAIC's RESS program** requires CLB 5 English minimum and valid work authorization.
6. **ISSofBC Welcome Centre temporary accommodation** is GARs only — not refugee claimants.
7. **Inland Refugee Society** manages a wait-list for refugee-specific housing in Vancouver — not all claimants can access housing directly.
8. **Toronto Rent Bank** does not serve persons receiving OW or ODSP; applicants must have a source of income and not exceed household income thresholds.
